## 2. Lenses

> **Lite:** 3 lenses · **Pro:** all 15 lenses

Lenses define two things above everything else: **how far or close the subject appears** and **how blurred the background gets**. The same camera with two different lenses produces images that feel completely different — one intimate and compressed, another wide and environmental. Choosing the right lens is often more impactful than choosing the right camera.

---

### Prime Lenses — Fixed focal length
#### 🎯 One focal length, one character — the purest optical tools

> **No camera experience needed:** A prime lens has a fixed zoom — you can't zoom in or out. That sounds like a limitation, but it's actually a feature: because the lens does only one thing, it does it exceptionally well. Prime lenses produce sharper images, more beautiful background blur, and a more distinctive visual character than zoom lenses. Each focal length has its own "personality" and is associated with a specific type of photography.

---

#### 01 · 24mm f/1.4 — Ultra-Wide Prime
**Technical:** Ultra-wide angle with a large maximum aperture. Captures an expansive field of view with slight perspective distortion at the edges. At f/1.4 it can produce background blur despite the wide angle. Exaggerates depth and spatial relationships — subjects close to the lens appear larger relative to the background.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** This lens fits a lot into the frame — whole rooms, wide landscapes, a person with their full environment behind them. Objects close to the camera look bigger and the background looks further away, which gives images a dramatic, almost cinematic depth. Used a lot in architecture, travel photography, and environmental portraits where the location is as important as the subject.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · 35mm f/1.4 — Wide Prime
**Technical:** Moderately wide angle with minimal distortion compared to ultra-wide lenses. Considered the closest focal length to natural human peripheral vision. At f/1.4 produces smooth, organic bokeh. A staple in photojournalism and street photography for its unobtrusive perspective.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** This is the "I was there" lens — it captures scenes the way your eyes actually experience them, with a natural, slightly wide perspective. It doesn't distort or compress. Feels honest and documentary. Perfect for street photography, lifestyle shots, and any image where you want the viewer to feel like they're in the scene rather than watching it from a distance.

`PRO`

---

#### 03 · 50mm f/1.2 — Standard Prime
**Technical:** The "normal" lens — its angle of view most closely matches the human eye's natural perspective. At f/1.2, one of the widest apertures available, producing exceptionally creamy bokeh with subject isolation. Renders colors and proportions without optical bias or distortion. The most versatile prime in existence.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The most natural-looking lens you can use. Photos taken with a 50mm look the way your eyes see the world — no exaggeration, no compression, just reality reproduced faithfully. The very wide f/1.2 aperture means the background melts into a beautiful blur while the subject stays razor-sharp. Works for portraits, products, food, street — almost everything. If you only had one lens, this would be it.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 04 · 85mm f/1.2 — Portrait Prime
**Technical:** The definitive portrait focal length. Produces a slight perspective compression that flatters facial features — it doesn't exaggerate the nose or widen the face the way wider lenses do. At f/1.2 the background blur is among the smoothest and most sought-after in photography. Requires distance from the subject, giving the subject space to be natural.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** This is the lens that makes people look their best. It has a slight compression effect that makes faces look more proportional and flattering — no distortion, no exaggeration of features. The background blur (bokeh) it produces is exceptionally smooth and creamy. If you've seen a portrait where the person is perfectly sharp and the background is a soft, beautiful blur, this lens probably created it. The go-to for beauty, fashion, and any portrait where the person is the entire story.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 05 · 100mm f/2.8 Macro — Macro Prime
**Technical:** Designed for 1:1 macro reproduction — the sensor captures the subject at true life size. Doubles as a medium telephoto portrait lens with excellent sharpness and smooth bokeh. Close focusing distance reveals textures invisible to the naked eye: fabric weave, skin texture, surface finishes, liquid droplets.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The lens that makes tiny things look massive and extraordinary. It gets close enough to a subject to photograph the texture of a lip, the detail of a watch mechanism, the surface of a perfume bottle cap, or a single water droplet. Used constantly in cosmetics, jewelry, food, and product photography where textures and materials need to look almost touchable. Also works beautifully as a portrait lens.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · 135mm f/2 — Telephoto Prime
**Technical:** Medium telephoto with exceptional optical quality. Produces strong perspective compression — the background appears to move closer to the subject, creating a more compact, layered composition. At f/2 the bokeh is extraordinarily smooth. Requires significant distance from the subject, which creates a natural, unaware quality in portraits.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** This lens compresses the scene — it makes the background appear much closer to the subject than it really is, creating a layered, cinematic look where everything feels stacked together beautifully. Because you have to stand far from your subject to use it, people in portraits often look more natural and relaxed — they don't feel the camera in their face. Produces one of the most beautiful background blurs of any lens. Very popular in fashion and editorial work.

`PRO`

---

### Zoom Lenses — Variable focal length
#### 🔄 Flexibility over character — the professional workhorses

> **No camera experience needed:** Zoom lenses let you adjust how close or far the subject appears without moving. They're more versatile than prime lenses but generally produce slightly less background blur and a less distinctive visual character. Professional zoom lenses, however, are exceptionally sharp and used constantly in commercial work where you need flexibility — events, weddings, advertising shoots where you can't always reposition yourself.

---

#### 07 · 16-35mm f/2.8 — Wide Zoom
**Technical:** Professional ultra-wide to wide zoom. Covers environmental and architectural photography through to moderate wide-angle portraits. f/2.8 constant aperture maintains consistent exposure throughout the zoom range. Ideal for interiors, real estate, event coverage, and any scene where fitting the full environment into the frame is the priority.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The "fit everything in" zoom. Covers wide angles that let you photograph entire rooms, dramatic landscapes, or a person surrounded by their full environment. ## 3. Lighting

> **Lite:** 4 light sources, 2 directions, no modifiers · **Pro:** all options

Lighting is the single most powerful variable in photography. The same subject, same camera, same lens — but different light — produces completely different emotional results.

---

### Light Sources
#### ☀️ Where the light comes from — natural vs. artificial

> **No camera experience needed:** The light source determines the overall mood and color temperature of the image. Natural light sources produce organic, emotionally resonant results. Artificial sources give you total control. The choice of light source is often what makes an image feel "real and human" vs. "polished and commercial."

---

#### 01 · Golden Hour
**Technical:** Natural sunlight at 1–2 hours after sunrise or before sunset. Color temperature 2,500–3,500K — extremely warm. Low angle produces long, soft shadows and strong directional light with minimal harsh contrast.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The most universally flattering light in nature. Everything looks golden and glowing — skin looks healthy, colors look rich, shadows are long and soft. This is the light you see in perfume ads and romantic portraits. Almost impossible to take a bad photo in golden hour. If you're not sure what to choose, start here.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · Overcast Diffused Daylight
**Technical:** Uniform, directionless light produced when clouds diffuse direct sunlight across the entire sky dome. Color temperature 6,000–7,500K. Produces virtually no harsh shadows. Maximum detail retention in both highlights and shadows simultaneously.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Cloudy day light is secretly a photographer's best friend. No harsh shadows anywhere — skin looks smooth and even, colors are accurate. It's the light makeup artists love for beauty shots. Not dramatic, but consistently flattering for almost anything.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 03 · Studio Softbox
**Technical:** Large artificial light source with a diffusion panel that spreads light evenly. Produces soft, directional light with gradual shadow transitions. Fully controllable in intensity, direction, and distance. The standard for commercial product and portrait photography.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A large artificial light in a box with a white diffusion panel — think of it as a miniature sun you can move wherever you want. Produces soft, professional-looking light with gentle shadows. This is what you see in most commercial photography — product shots, professional headshots, cosmetics campaigns.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 04 · Hard Direct Sunlight
**Technical:** Unfiltered direct sunlight at midday. Color temperature 5,500–6,500K. Produces extremely high contrast with sharp, well-defined shadows. Creates strong highlights and deep shadows simultaneously.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Harsh midday sun — used intentionally, it creates incredibly graphic, high-contrast images with sharp black shadows and bright highlights. Think fashion photography in the desert or street photography in a sun-drenched city. Not flattering for skin, but powerful for mood and drama.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Rembrandt Lighting
**Technical:** Single light source at 45° to the side and slightly above, creating a small triangle of light on the shadowed cheek. Ratio between lit and shadow side typically 3:1 to 5:1. Produces strong three-dimensionality and depth.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Named after the painter — one side of the face is well lit, the other mostly in shadow, with a small triangle of light on the darker cheek. Makes faces look three-dimensional, sculpted, and dramatic. Used in fine art portraits and character studies. Makes people look thoughtful, powerful, or mysterious.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · Split Lighting
**Technical:** Light source at exactly 90° to the subject, dividing it precisely in half — one side fully illuminated, the other in complete shadow. Produces maximum contrast with a stark, graphic quality.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The light divides the subject exactly down the middle — one half bright white, the other pitch black. One of the most dramatically striking setups in photography. Used in rock musician portraits, edgy fashion, and any image that needs to feel bold and intense.

`PRO`

---

#### 07 · Rim / Backlight
**Technical:** Light source positioned behind or slightly behind the subject. Creates a bright outline around the silhouette, separating the subject from the background. Often combined with a front fill light for a balanced exposure.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Light from behind creates a glowing outline around the subject — hair, shoulders, and edges all glow. Creates an ethereal, cinematic look. Used constantly in film, fashion, and beauty photography. Also produces silhouettes when there's no front fill.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 08 · Neon / Colored Ambient
**Technical:** Artificial colored light sources (neon tubes, RGB LEDs, colored gels) as primary illumination. Creates strong color casts across the subject. Associated with cyberpunk, editorial fashion, and contemporary commercial aesthetics.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Vibrant colored light — pink, blue, purple, green — washing over the subject. Think neon signs of a rainy city street at night. Creates a bold, modern, slightly futuristic look very popular in contemporary fashion and beauty photography.

`PRO`

---

#### 09 · Candlelight / Firelight
**Technical:** Single-point warm light source, color temperature 1,800–2,200K. Very low intensity. Produces deep shadows with a small, intimate pool of warm light. Rapid light falloff. The most organic and emotionally intimate light source available.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The warm, flickering light of a candle or open fire. A small pool of golden-orange light surrounded by deep shadows — intimate, dramatic, and ancient-feeling. Used for intimate portraits, fine dining, and any image that needs to feel human, vulnerable, or quietly beautiful.

`PRO`

---

#### 10 · Ring Light
**Technical:** Circular light source mounted around the lens axis. Produces shadowless frontal illumination with a distinctive circular catchlight in the eyes. Very flat, even light. Standard in beauty, makeup, and content creator photography.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A circular light around the camera lens — no shadows anywhere, perfectly even skin tone, and a distinctive circular reflection in the subject's eyes. You recognize it immediately from beauty tutorials and Instagram portraits. Very flattering for skin and cosmetics. Clean, glowing, very "internet era."

`PRO`

### Light Direction & Quality
#### 🧭 Where the light hits — angle and hardness

> **No camera experience needed:** Direction creates shadows, and shadows create the illusion of three-dimensionality. A face lit from directly in front looks flat; the same face lit from 45° to the side suddenly has depth, bone structure, and dimension.

---

#### 01 · Frontal / Flat
**Technical:** Light source aligned with the camera axis. Produces minimal shadows and very low contrast. Maximum detail with no three-dimensionality. Standard for packshots and any application requiring clinical accuracy.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Light straight in front — no shadows, everything evenly lit. Looks clean and accurate but flat. Good for product shots where you want to show exactly what something looks like. Think ID photos or e-commerce product images.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · 45° Side (Classic Portrait)
**Technical:** Light at 45° horizontally and vertically above the subject. Produces soft shadows that reveal facial structure while maintaining shadow detail. The most universally flattering direction for human subjects.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The sweet spot of portrait lighting — like sunlight through a window in the afternoon. Creates gentle shadows that give the face shape without being dramatic. This is the default "looks good on everyone" direction. Most professional portrait studios start here.

`LITE` `PRO`

#### 03 · 90° Hard Side
**Technical:** Light at exactly 90° to the subject, creating a 50/50 split of light and shadow. Sharp shadow edge. Maximum texture revelation — any surface irregularity becomes clearly visible.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Light directly from the side — one half lit, one half dark. Creates strong shadows that make textures really stand out: wood grain, stone, fabric weave. On faces it's very sculptural and dramatic. Used in character portraits and any image emphasizing material texture.

`PRO`

---

#### 04 · Top-Down / Overhead
**Technical:** Light source directly above pointing downward. Creates deep shadows in eye sockets and under features. Ideal for flat lay photography and overhead product shots.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Light from directly above — like a spotlight on a stage. Dramatic on people, but perfect for flat lay photography (products and food shot from above). Everything is evenly lit with clean, minimal shadows. Very common in food photography and product catalogues.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Under-Lighting
**Technical:** Light source below the subject pointing upward — inverse of natural light. Produces shadows opposite to natural expectation. Creates an unnatural, disorienting effect used for surreal or conceptual imagery.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Light from below — completely unnatural, since no light in nature illuminates from the ground up. Creates an unsettling, slightly eerie effect because our brains expect light from above. Used intentionally for surreal editorial images where "wrong" feels right.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · Wraparound Soft
**Technical:** Multiple diffused sources or a very large single source illuminating from several directions simultaneously. Minimizes all shadows. Standard in high-end beauty and skincare photography where even the subtlest shadow is undesirable.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Light that seems to come from everywhere at once, wrapping gently around the subject with almost no shadows. The most flattering light possible for skin — makes pores disappear, evens complexion, creates a luminous glow. Used constantly in luxury skincare campaigns and beauty close-ups.

`LITE` `PRO`

### Light Modifiers
#### 🎛️ Fine-tuning the light — tools that shape and control

> **No camera experience needed:** Modifiers are accessories that change the quality or effect of a light source. Each adds a specific element to the image that would be difficult or impossible to achieve otherwise.

---

#### 01 · Catchlights
**Technical:** Small specular highlights reflected in the subject's eyes from the light source. Essential in portrait and beauty — subjects without catchlights appear lifeless. Their shape and position reveal the lighting setup used.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The tiny spark of light in someone's eyes that makes them look alive and engaged. Every portrait photographer makes sure these are present — without them, even a technically perfect portrait feels something is missing. A small detail with enormous emotional impact.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · Fill Light / Reflector
**Technical:** Secondary light or reflective surface that reduces contrast by adding light to the shadow side. Controls the relationship between highlights and shadows without altering the primary light's character.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A secondary light that softens the dark shadows created by the main light. The difference between a portrait that looks natural and balanced vs. one that looks overly dramatic often comes down to how much fill light was used.

`PRO`

---

#### 03 · Hair Light
**Technical:** Dedicated light source aimed at the subject's hair from above or behind. Separates hair from background by adding brightness and sheen. Standard in beauty and fashion photography.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A small separate light aimed just at the hair, making it glow and shimmer. That healthy, luminous hair sheen in shampoo ads is almost always a dedicated hair light. Without it, dark hair against a dark background can disappear completely.

`PRO`

---

#### 04 · Gobo / Shadow Patterns
**Technical:** Opaque panel or patterned screen placed between light source and subject to project patterns — venetian blind shadows, leaf patterns, geometric shapes. Adds visual complexity without requiring an actual physical set.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A panel with patterns cut into it that projects interesting shadow patterns — like venetian blind shadows across a face, or leaf patterns suggesting a forest. Adds atmosphere and environmental storytelling to a studio portrait without needing to actually be in that location.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Volumetric Light Rays
**Technical:** Light rays made visible by suspended particles — dust, smoke, fog, haze. Creates visible shafts of light traveling through space, adding atmospheric depth and strong three-dimensionality.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Those beautiful beams of light cutting through dust or fog — the kind that appear in a forest at morning or through the windows of an old church. They make light a physical presence in the image. Immediately cinematic and dramatic.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · Practical Lights
**Technical:** Light sources visible within the frame — table lamps, candles, neon signs, screens — that serve simultaneously as props and as actual contributing light sources. Create naturalistic, lived-in quality and establish setting and context.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Light sources you can actually see in the photo — a lamp, a candle, a neon sign. They make the lighting feel natural and real because you can see where it's coming from. A portrait lit by a visible bedside lamp feels completely different from the same portrait in a studio — more intimate, more personal.

`PRO`

## 4. Composition & Framing

> **Lite:** 4 shot types, 2 angles · **Pro:** all options

Composition is the decision of what to include in the frame, where to place the subject, and from what angle to view the scene. It's the difference between a photo that feels random and one that feels intentional and powerful.

---

### Shot Types / Framing
#### 📐 How much of the subject you see — distance and intimacy

> **No camera experience needed:** Shot type determines how close or far the camera is from the subject — and that distance changes the emotional relationship between the viewer and the subject. Close shots feel intimate and personal. Wide shots feel epic or lonely.

---

#### 01 · Extreme Close-Up (ECU)
**Technical:** Frames only a specific detail — an eye, a mouth, a texture, a product detail. Fills the entire frame with a fragment. Eliminates all context and background. Standard in cosmetics, jewelry, and any image where a specific detail is the entire message.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** So close that only one specific detail fills the frame — just an eye, just lips, just a watch clasp. Zero background. Used in beauty ads for lipstick color, jewelry ads for diamond brilliance, food photography for texture. When the detail IS the story.

`PRO`

---

#### 02 · Close-Up (CU)
**Technical:** Frames the subject's face tightly — typically from chin to top of head. Strong emotional connection and immediate recognition of expression. The standard in beauty, portrait, and product hero shots.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Face filling most of the frame — full expression, every detail of eyes and skin. The framing of album covers and magazine covers. Creates immediate emotional connection — the viewer feels like they know this person or are inches from the product.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 03 · Medium Close-Up (MCU)
**Technical:** Subject from mid-chest upward. Balances facial detail with body language. The standard news, interview, and casual portrait framing.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The most natural and comfortable framing — like talking to someone across a café table. You see their face and enough of their upper body to read body language. Used in most professional headshots and LinkedIn portraits.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 04 · Medium Shot (MS)
**Technical:** Subject from approximately the waist upward. Shows facial expression, arm gestures, and upper body. The most versatile and commonly used framing in commercial portraiture.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Waist up — face, expression, arms, and a hint of environment. Works for almost any portrait or lifestyle situation. The safe, versatile default.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 05 · Full Body / Full Length
**Technical:** Complete subject from head to toe. Essential in fashion to show complete outfits, in sports to show athletic form, and any image where the subject's full physical presence matters.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Head to toe — the complete person. You see the full outfit, posture, and the way they carry themselves. Standard in fashion photography. Less intimate than close-ups but essential when the whole physical story matters.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 06 · Wide / Environmental
**Technical:** Subject occupies a small portion of the frame, surrounded by significant environment. The setting becomes a narrative element equal in importance to the subject.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The subject is relatively small, surrounded by their environment. The location tells as much of the story as the person. Think a lone figure on a vast beach or a chef in a busy kitchen. About the relationship between a person and their world.

`PRO`

---

#### 07 · Aerial / Bird's Eye
**Technical:** Camera directly above the subject looking straight down. Eliminates depth cues and produces a flat, graphic, map-like perspective. Standard for flat lay photography, food, and urban landscape.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Straight down from above — everything becomes a flat graphic element. The standard for flat lay photography: products or food arranged on a surface and shot from overhead. Very popular for Instagram and any image where arrangement and pattern are the point.

`PRO`

---

#### 08 · Worm's Eye / Low Angle
**Technical:** Camera at or near ground level, pointing upward. Makes subjects appear towering and imposing. Exaggerates height relative to the frame.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Camera at ground level looking up — makes everything look tall and powerful. A person photographed this way looks like a giant. Used in superhero photography, architecture, and fashion that emphasizes confidence and authority.

`PRO`

`LITE` `PRO`

#### 04 · Dutch Angle

## 5. Exposure Settings

> **Lite:** Aperture only (3 values) · **Pro:** Aperture + ISO + Shutter Speed (full range)

Exposure determines whether an image is bright or dark, sharp or blurred, clean or grainy. Three settings work together — aperture, shutter speed, and ISO — forming the "exposure triangle." Each setting also has a creative effect beyond controlling light.

---

### Aperture (f-stop)
#### 🔆 Controls background blur and depth of field

> **No camera experience needed:** Aperture is the opening inside the lens. A wide opening (low f-number like f/1.2) blurs the background beautifully. A narrow opening (high f-number like f/16) keeps everything sharp. This is one of the most powerful creative tools — the difference between a subject that pops off the background and one that merges with it.

| Value | Technical | In Plain English | Available |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **f/1.2** | Maximum aperture on fast primes. Extremely shallow depth of field — only millimeters in focus at close range. | Background is a completely smooth blur. Only the exact focus point is sharp — everything else melts away. Most sought-after effect in portrait photography. | `LITE` `PRO` |
| **f/1.8** | Very wide aperture. Shallow DOF with slightly more tolerance than f/1.2. | Beautiful background blur, slightly more forgiving. Still isolates the subject effectively. The sweet spot for portrait photographers. | `LITE` `PRO` |
| **f/2.8** | Standard maximum aperture for professional zoom lenses. Shallow DOF across a wider focus range. | Good background blur with more of the subject in focus. Standard for event and wedding photographers. | `LITE` `PRO` |
| **f/4** | Medium aperture. Moderate depth of field. Excellent sharpness on most lenses. | Subject clearly separated from background but background not completely blurred. Good for portraits with environmental context. | `PRO` |
| **f/5.6** | Aperture where most lenses reach optical resolution peak. Moderate depth of field. | The sharpness sweet spot for most lenses. Standard for group photos and environmental portraits. | `PRO` |
| **f/8** | Narrow aperture. Deep depth of field — most of the scene in focus front to back. | Everything from near to far is sharp. Standard for landscape photography and architecture. | `PRO` |
| **f/11** | Very narrow aperture. Near-total depth of field. Standard in landscape photography. | Almost everything in the entire scene is in sharp focus. | `PRO` |
| **f/16** | Extreme aperture. Total depth of field. Diffraction begins to slightly reduce peak sharpness. | Everything in the scene is sharp. Used for maximum depth of field in technical and architectural photography. | `PRO` |

---

### ISO (Sensor Sensitivity)
#### 🌙 Controls grain and low-light capability

> **No camera experience needed:** ISO determines how sensitive the camera is to light. Low ISO produces clean, smooth images. High ISO allows shooting in near-darkness but adds visible grain. Grain isn't always bad — sometimes it creates a beautiful analog, film-like quality.

| Value | Technical | In Plain English | Available |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **ISO 100** | Base ISO. Minimum noise, maximum dynamic range, cleanest possible file. | The cleanest possible image — perfectly smooth, no grain. Needs decent light. Standard for studio and product photography. | `LITE` `PRO` |
| **ISO 400** | Low-mid ISO. Very clean on modern sensors. Workable in moderately low light. | Essentially clean to the naked eye. Go-to for overcast outdoor and indoor scenes with good ambient light. | `LITE` `PRO` |
| **ISO 800** | Mid ISO. Slight noise visible at 100%. Usable in indoor ambient light. | Very slight grain visible if you zoom in, but clean at normal viewing. Good for indoor events. | `PRO` |
| **ISO 1600** | High ISO. Noticeable grain. Requires good noise reduction. | Visible grain that starts to give images a slightly analog, atmospheric quality. Used for dim indoor conditions. | `PRO` |
| **ISO 3200** | Very high ISO. Strong grain. Film-like quality on modern sensors. | The "analog film" zone — grain is clearly visible and becomes a creative texture. Creates moody, intimate images. | `PRO` |
| **ISO 6400+** | Extreme ISO. Heavy grain. Used when capturing the moment matters more than technical quality. | Maximum grain — the image looks documentary, raw, and immediate. The look of warzone photography and underground concerts. | `PRO` |

---

### Motion & Time Style
#### ⏱️ How movement appears in the image — from perfectly still to flowing through time

> **No camera experience needed:** This is not about technical numbers — it's about choosing what you want movement to *look like* in the image. Should everything be perfectly frozen and sharp? Should water flow like silk? Should city lights leave glowing trails? Each option produces a distinct visual result that changes the entire mood of the photograph.

---

#### 01 · Perfectly Still
**Technical:** High shutter speed equivalent (1/500s+). All motion completely arrested. Every element in the scene sharp and defined, with no indication of movement whatsoever.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The world frozen in time — every element sharp and still, as if everything stopped. Used for portraits, products, architecture, and any image where clarity and sharpness matter more than a sense of motion. The default look for most professional photography.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · Frozen Action
**Technical:** Ultra-high shutter speed equivalent (1/2000s–1/8000s). Arrests fast movement that would otherwise be invisible to the naked eye — athletes at peak exertion, water droplets mid-splash, fabric caught in a gust of wind.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Stops action so fast it reveals moments the human eye can't see — a basketball player suspended in mid-air at peak jump, a water balloon bursting, hair caught in a sudden wind. Creates images with incredible energy and impact because they freeze reality at a speed we can't normally perceive. Essential for sports, action, and any image with explosive movement.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 03 · Silky Water / Smooth Flow
**Technical:** Long exposure equivalent (0.5s–4s). Flowing water loses its individual droplet definition and becomes a smooth, continuous silk-like surface. All other stationary elements remain sharp. The contrast between the fluid water and the solid environment is the defining visual feature.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Waterfalls, rivers, ocean waves, and rain all turn into a smooth, flowing silk texture — beautiful and dreamlike, like liquid glass. Everything else in the scene stays sharp. The look of fine art landscape photography and premium travel campaigns. Immediately makes an image feel serene, elegant, and timeless.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 04 · Light Trails
**Technical:** Very long exposure equivalent (2s–30s) in low-light conditions with moving light sources. Stationary elements render normally while moving lights (vehicles, flashlights, sparklers) trace continuous glowing lines through the frame.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** City traffic becomes rivers of red and white light flowing through the streets. A sparkler held by a child becomes a glowing circle drawn in the air. Fireworks become full blooming flowers of light. The environment is static and sharp while light itself paints through it over time. The signature look of night city photography and long-exposure urban imagery.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Motion Blur
**Technical:** Medium shutter speed equivalent (1/30s–1/15s) applied to a moving subject. Creates directional blur that follows the subject's movement path, implying speed and kinetic energy while retaining enough form to identify the subject.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The subject is moving fast enough that it leaves a blur trail in the direction of movement — a cyclist becomes a streak of speed and color, a dancer's arm becomes a sweeping arc, a person walking becomes slightly ghostly. The image feels alive with movement and energy. Very used in sports photography and editorial fashion when the goal is to convey energy rather than freeze a perfect moment.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · Star Trails
**Technical:** Extreme long exposure equivalent (15 minutes–several hours). The Earth's rotation causes stars to appear as curved arcs in the sky while the landscape below remains sharp. Creates concentric circular trails centered on the celestial pole.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The night sky becomes alive with curved streaks of light — stars trace perfect circular arcs across the sky as the Earth rotates beneath them, all centered on the North Star. The landscape below is sharp and still. One of the most visually spectacular effects in photography — it literally shows the movement of the planet. Used in astrophotography, nature, and any image meant to feel cosmic and awe-inspiring.

`PRO`

---

#### 07 · Ghost / Double Motion
**Technical:** Long exposure combined with subject movement or deliberate repositioning. The subject appears multiple times within the same frame at different stages of movement — partially transparent and overlapping. Creates a surreal, painterly quality.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The subject appears as a semi-transparent ghost — a dancer photographed through a slow exposure appears in three positions at once, overlapping and translucent. Creates a dreamy, surreal quality that makes movement itself visible across time within a single image. Used in fine art photography, dance, and any conceptual image where the passage of time and motion is the subject.

`PRO`

## 6. Color & Style

> **Lite:** 3 color grades, 2 techniques, 1 extra · **Pro:** all options

Color grade and visual style are the final creative layer. Two photos taken with identical settings in the same location can look completely different depending on how color is treated. Style choices signal genre, era, emotional register, and intended audience.

---

### Color Grades
#### 🎨 The color palette of the image — mood through tone

> **No camera experience needed:** Color grading adjusts the colors of an image to create a specific mood. Think of it as the difference between a warm vintage film and a cold, clinical digital image. The same photo with different color grades tells completely different emotional stories.

---

#### 01 · Natural / True-to-Life
**Technical:** Minimal color manipulation. Colors reproduce as accurately as possible. White balance neutral, contrast moderate, saturation faithful to the scene. Required for commercial work where color accuracy is a business requirement.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** What you see is what was there — honest, accurate colors with no creative tinting. Skin looks like real skin, grass looks green. Used whenever accurate color representation matters more than mood: products, food, real estate.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · Warm / Golden
**Technical:** Positive shift in color temperature towards 3,000–4,500K. Increased orange and yellow in midtones. Slightly lifted shadows with warm tint. Associated with analog film stocks and golden hour light.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Everything shifted toward golden, warm tones — the image feels like late afternoon sun even if it wasn't. Skin looks healthy and glowing, environments feel inviting. The color grade of travel photography, lifestyle brands, and food photography.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 03 · Cool / Cinematic Blue
**Technical:** Shift towards 7,000–9,000K. Increased blue and cyan in shadows and midtones. Often paired with reduced saturation in highlights. The signature grade of contemporary action cinema and commercial advertising.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Everything shifted toward cool blues and teals — crisp, modern, and slightly cinematic. The look of contemporary action films and tech brand advertising. Used in automotive photography, sports campaigns, and anything that needs to feel sharp and current.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 04 · Orange & Teal
**Technical:** Simultaneous push of skin tones toward orange/amber while shifting shadows toward teal/cyan. Creates maximum complementary contrast between warm subjects and cool environments. The dominant commercial color grade of the last 15 years.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The most famous color grade in commercial photography — warm orange skin tones against cool teal backgrounds. You've seen this in almost every blockbuster film and major advertising campaign. It's so common because it's so effective: the contrast makes subjects pop dramatically and reads as "big budget" immediately.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Black & White / Monochrome
**Technical:** Complete desaturation with tonal adjustments to channel mixing. High-contrast B&W emphasizes structure and drama. Low-contrast creates a soft, timeless quality. Communicates entirely through light, shadow, and form.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** No color — just light and shadow. Removing color forces focus on shapes, textures, and expressions. Images often feel more timeless and emotionally serious. A portrait in black and white feels like a different kind of document — more permanent, more elemental.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · Faded / Matte Film
**Technical:** Raised black point (shadows lifted to gray), reduced contrast, slightly desaturated palette, often with a subtle color tint in shadows. Mimics aged photographic film or expired film stock.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The "faded photo" look — blacks aren't fully black, everything has a slightly washed-out, vintage quality. Feels like a real photograph sitting in a drawer for years — analog, human, slightly imperfect. Very popular in indie fashion and portrait photography.

`PRO`

---

#### 07 · High Contrast / Dramatic
**Technical:** Expanded contrast with deep blacks and bright whites. Compressed midtones. Increased local contrast. Shadows block up while highlights blow out selectively. Standard in sports, automotive, and bold fashion photography.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Blacks are deep, whites are bright, everything is punchy and vivid. The image jumps off the screen with energy. Used for sports campaigns, action photography, and any image designed to stop a scroll or dominate a billboard.

`PRO`

---

#### 08 · Desaturated / Moody
**Technical:** Significant reduction in overall saturation with selective color preservation. Muted, almost monochromatic palette. Often paired with cool shadows and reduced contrast. Associated with contemporary fine art and premium lifestyle brands.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Colors are muted and quiet — not black and white, but close. Subdued and introspective, like a rainy afternoon. Very fashionable in premium lifestyle brands and Scandinavian aesthetics. The mood is cool, considered, and sophisticated.

`PRO`

---

### Visual Techniques & Style
#### 🎬 The overall aesthetic — from hyperreal to analog to cinematic

> **No camera experience needed:** Beyond color, images have an overall visual style that signals their purpose and aesthetic category. A hyperrealistic image and a film grain image can have identical settings — but one looks like a clinical digital capture and the other feels like it was shot on 35mm film in 1978.

---

#### 01 · Photorealistic / Hyperrealistic
**Technical:** Maximum technical fidelity. Full resolution detail, accurate color, physically correct lighting behavior. The AI produces an image indistinguishable from an actual photograph taken with the specified equipment.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** As real as possible — looks like an actual photograph, not an illustration or render. Every texture, shadow, and light reflection behaves exactly as it would in the physical world. The default mode for commercial work where the image needs to be believable.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · Film Grain / Analog
**Technical:** Simulation of silver halide grain from photochemical film stocks (35mm ISO 400–3200). Organic grain structure larger in shadows, combined with slight halation, reduced sharpness, and color characteristics of specific film stocks (Kodak Portra, Fuji Superia, Ilford HP5).

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Looks like it was shot on real film — grain, slight softness, and the warm imperfections of analog photography. The look of photos from the 70s, 80s, and 90s. Enormously popular because digital images can feel overly perfect and cold. Film grain brings humanity and warmth back.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 03 · Editorial / Magazine
**Technical:** Polished, intentional composition with deliberate art direction. Clean retouching, controlled color palette, strong graphic sensibility. Designed to work as a printed magazine page with high contrast and minimal visual clutter.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The look of a professional magazine spread — everything feels intentional, polished, and art-directed. Nothing is accidental. When you see this look, your brain immediately reads "expensive publication." Used for fashion, luxury brands, and anything that needs to communicate premium quality.

`PRO`

---

#### 04 · Documentary / Candid
**Technical:** Naturalistic aesthetic prioritizing authenticity over technical perfection. Slightly imperfect compositions, natural available light, subjects unaware or mid-movement, minimal post-processing. The visual language of photojournalism.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Real, unposed, caught-in-the-moment. Everything that makes a "perfect" commercial photo is absent — no styling, no posing, no controlled lighting. But there's something more valuable: it feels true. Used when authenticity matters more than perfection.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Fine Art
**Technical:** Image conceived as an autonomous artistic statement. Strong conceptual intent, deliberate compositional choices that may violate commercial conventions, often with distinctive post-processing that becomes a signature style. Designed for gallery walls.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Photography as art — meant to be hung on a wall and contemplated, not used to sell something. Has a strong personal point of view and often breaks the rules intentionally. The goal isn't to show a product clearly — it's to create an image that makes you feel or think something specific.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · Fashion / Commercial
**Technical:** High-production-value aesthetic optimized for commercial application. Impeccable retouching, technically flawless execution, color palette calibrated for brand guidelines. Designed to work across billboard, magazine, and social media simultaneously.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** High-budget, high-production commercial photography. Everything is perfect: skin is flawless, colors are deliberate, composition is strong. The look of H&M, Nike, Zara, Chanel campaigns. Every element is controlled and intentional. Maximum visual impact with complete technical mastery.

`LITE` `PRO`

### Technical Extras
#### ⚙️ Specific technical effects that modify the image at a fundamental level

---

#### 01 · 8K / Ultra-High Resolution
**Technical:** Maximum detail density — equivalent to 8K digital cinema standard. Fine textures, micro-details, and subtle gradations preserved at the finest possible level.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Maximum detail in every corner — every texture is visible. You could print this image at poster size and it would still look sharp up close. Used for luxury product photography and images that need to work at large scale.

`LITE` `PRO`

---

#### 02 · HDR (High Dynamic Range)
**Technical:** Extended dynamic range retaining detail simultaneously in deep shadows and bright highlights — beyond standard photography capture. Produces a technically "impossible" single-exposure photograph.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** In a normal photo, you usually have to choose between exposing for bright areas or dark areas. HDR keeps both: deep shadows have detail AND bright highlights have detail simultaneously. Used in real estate, landscape, and any situation where losing detail is unacceptable.

`PRO`

---

#### 03 · Long Exposure Effect
**Technical:** Simulated long exposure — motion blur on all moving elements while static elements remain sharp. Creates temporal layering where time becomes visible within the frame.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Anything that moved becomes blurred or ghostly — waterfalls turn silky, traffic becomes light trails — while everything stationary stays sharp. Immediately recognizable and visually stunning, especially with water and city lights at night.

`PRO`

---

#### 04 · Double Exposure
**Technical:** Two separate images composited within a single frame. Can be graphic (portrait with landscape within the silhouette) or surreal (objects merging into each other). Creates imagery impossible in a single capture.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Two images layered into one — like a portrait with a forest visible inside the silhouette. Creates a dreamlike, surreal quality where two realities coexist. Popular in conceptual photography that wants to suggest an inner world or a poetic connection between a person and a place.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Freeze Frame / High Speed
**Technical:** Ultra-high-speed flash simulation freezing motion at 1/10,000s or faster — beyond mechanical camera limits. Captures transient events invisible to the human eye.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Freezes motion so fast that things invisible to the human eye become visible — a water droplet forming a perfect crown, a burst of powder exploding outward. Images have an otherworldly quality because they show reality at a timescale humans can't perceive.

`PRO`

## 8. Intended Use — Web & Social Media

> **Pro only** — This section instructs the AI to compose the image with a specific digital placement in mind. The same subject photographed for a hero header vs. an Instagram story requires completely different compositional decisions — aspect ratio, where the subject sits, how much empty space exists and where, and what emotional register drives the viewer toward an action.

Activating this section adds a composition brief to the prompt that overrides generic framing in favor of platform-specific intent.

---

### 8.1 Web Placement
#### 🖥️ Where on the website this image will live

> **In plain English:** Different parts of a website have different dimensions, different scroll behaviors, and different jobs. A hero image needs to work at full browser width — sometimes 2500px wide — with space for a headline and a button sitting on top of it. A card thumbnail needs to work at 400px square. Telling the AI where the image will live means it composes with that specific container in mind.

---

#### 01 · Hero / Full-Width Header
**Technical:** Landscape orientation, aspect ratio 16:9 to 21:9. Subject positioned to allow headline text and CTA overlay without obscuring the primary focal point. High visual impact required — this is the first thing a visitor sees. Negative space intentionally distributed to accommodate UI elements. Background must remain readable under semi-transparent overlays.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The big full-screen image at the top of a website. Needs to be wide, dramatic, and still work with text sitting on top of it — which means part of the image has to be clean enough for words to be legible. The job of this image is to make someone want to stay on the site.

`PRO`

---

#### 02 · Content Section Background
**Technical:** Full-width background image for an interior content section. Subject treatment more subtle than hero — typically lower contrast or desaturated to ensure foreground content remains readable. Often used with a color overlay at 30–60% opacity. Composition must work at multiple aspect ratios as section height is variable.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A background image behind a block of content further down the page — not the hero, but a section that needs visual texture or atmosphere. Often slightly muted or dark so text sitting on top is readable. Less aggressive than a hero, more atmospheric.

`PRO`

---

#### 03 · Card / Thumbnail
**Technical:** Square or slightly portrait aspect ratio (1:1 to 4:5). Subject centered or positioned in the upper two-thirds to survive cropping by various CMS grid systems. Immediate legibility at small sizes — detail that reads well at 400px. Strong focal point with minimal peripheral complexity.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The small image that represents a blog post, product, or team member in a grid. Needs to work at thumbnail size — the subject has to be immediately recognizable even when the image is small. Less negative space, more subject-forward composition.

`PRO`

---

#### 04 · Banner / Leaderboard
**Technical:** Extreme landscape aspect ratio (3:1 to 8:1). Very limited vertical space — subject must be legible within a narrow horizontal strip. Either a tight crop of a key detail or a wide environmental shot where the subject occupies the center third. Text space typically on left or right side.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A very wide, very short strip — like the banner at the top or bottom of a page, or a promotional bar. Extremely horizontal. The image needs to tell its story within a narrow horizontal slice. Faces cropped to eyes and lips, products shown from their most recognizable angle, landscapes shown as a thin panoramic strip.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · Favicon / Avatar / Profile
**Technical:** Square format, extreme close-up crop. Subject must be immediately recognizable as a 32×32px icon. Simple, high-contrast, unambiguous. Faces cropped tightly, logos isolated on clean background, symbols simplified to their most essential form.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** A tiny square image that represents a brand, person, or concept — the size of a browser tab icon or a profile photo. Must be instantly readable at very small sizes. Maximum simplicity, maximum contrast, zero ambiguity about what it is.

`PRO`

---

### 8.2 Social Media Placement
#### 📱 Platform-specific composition — each network has its own visual language

> **In plain English:** Every social media platform has different image dimensions, different scroll behaviors, and different audience expectations. A LinkedIn image looks and feels different from a TikTok thumbnail. This section makes the AI compose with the specific platform in mind — not just the dimensions, but the visual style that performs on each.

#### 01 · Feed Post — Square (1:1)
**Technical:** 1080×1080px. The universal format — works across Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn feeds without cropping. Subject centered with sufficient margin on all sides to survive any platform's safe zone. Must have strong visual impact at mobile screen width (~375px).

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The classic square Instagram post. Works everywhere without cropping. Subject centered, visually balanced on all sides. Must look good on a phone screen — bold, clear, and immediately striking when someone is scrolling at speed.

`PRO`

---

#### 02 · Feed Post — Portrait (4:5)
**Technical:** 1080×1350px. Takes up maximum vertical real estate in a mobile feed — the largest format allowed before Instagram switches to a different display mode. Subject positioned in upper two-thirds. The dominant format for high-impact organic feed posts.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Slightly taller than square — takes up more of someone's phone screen when scrolling, which means more attention. The format serious content creators use when they want to dominate the feed. Subject in the upper portion, slightly more breathing room at the bottom.

`PRO`

---

#### 03 · Stories / Reels Cover — Vertical (9:16)
**Technical:** 1080×1920px. Full-screen vertical format. Subject must be centered in the middle third to avoid being obscured by UI elements (username at top, CTA/swipe at bottom). High energy, immediate impact — stories are consumed at speed with a single tap to advance.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** Full-screen vertical — fills the entire phone screen. The format of Instagram Stories, TikTok, and Reels covers. The subject must be in the center of the frame because the top and bottom have UI elements overlaid. Needs to grab attention in under a second. The most immersive and demanding format.

`PRO`

---

#### 04 · Cover / Profile Banner
**Technical:** Wide landscape format (16:9 to 4:1 depending on platform — Facebook 820×312px, Twitter/X 1500×500px, LinkedIn 1584×396px). Subject positioned to avoid the profile picture circle that overlaps the left side on most platforms. Environmental or atmospheric imagery works better than tight subject shots.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The wide banner behind a profile photo. The challenge here is that a circular profile picture will overlap the lower-left corner — so the subject or key visual element must not sit there. Works best with environmental or atmospheric images that can absorb that overlap without losing their impact.

`PRO`

---

#### 05 · LinkedIn Banner
**Technical:** 1584×396px — extremely wide and shallow (4:1). Professional context. Subject or environment must convey credibility, expertise, or industry context. Text-safe zone in the right two-thirds (left portion partially obscured by profile photo). Tone must be consistent with professional networking context.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** LinkedIn's banner is extra-wide and very shallow — and the left side is partially covered by the profile photo. The image needs to look professional and credible, not playful or casual. Works best with clean environmental shots, architectural images, or subtle branded imagery that doesn't need to be fully visible to work.

`PRO`

---

#### 06 · YouTube Thumbnail
**Technical:** 1280×720px (16:9). The highest-stakes small image in digital media — a thumbnail competes with dozens of others on the same screen and must generate a click. High contrast, bold colors, expressive faces, or dramatic compositions. Text overlay space typically on left or right third. Legible at 200px width.

> 🗣️ **In plain English:** The image people see before deciding whether to watch your video. It has one job: make someone click. High contrast, expressive, slightly dramatic — YouTube thumbnails intentionally push toward the edge of sensationalism because they have to compete with every other thumbnail on the screen. Must read clearly when small.

`PRO`

### 8.3 Negative Space
#### ⬜ Empty space — intentional room for text, UI, and breathing

> **In plain English:** Negative space is the empty area in an image — the sky above a subject, the plain wall to their left, the dark floor below a product. For web and social use, negative space isn't empty — it's where your headline, button, logo, or price tag will live. Telling the AI where you need empty space means the composition is built around your UI, not fighting against it.

---

#### Direction

| Option | What it produces | Pro |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Left side clear** | Subject positioned right — empty space on the left for text or UI | ✅ |
| **Right side clear** | Subject positioned left — empty space on the right | ✅ |
| **Top clear** | Subject in lower portion — sky or clean background above for headline | ✅ |
| **Bottom clear** | Subject in upper portion — clean space below for CTA or caption | ✅ |
| **Center clear** | Subject pushed to edges — center of frame open for text overlay | ✅ |
| **Subject centered, edges free** | Subject in center — negative space distributed symmetrically around it | ✅ |

---

#### Amount

| Option | Approximate empty area | Best used for | Pro |
|--------|----------------------|---------------|-----|
| **Subtle** | ~20% of frame | Atmospheric background, minimal text | ✅ |
| **Moderate** | ~40% of frame | Standard hero with headline + subtext | ✅ |
| **Generous** | ~60% of frame | Large text blocks, multiple UI elements | ✅ |
| **Extreme** | Subject in one third, rest empty | Full-page text-heavy layouts | ✅ |

### 8.4 Overlay Readiness
#### 🔲 Preparing the image for UI elements sitting on top

> **In plain English:** When text, buttons, or logos will sit on top of an image, the area underneath needs to be clean enough for them to be readable. This tells the AI to keep specific zones of the image simple, low-contrast, and dark or light enough for overlaid elements to work.

| Option | What the AI does | Pro |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Text overlay area** | Keeps the designated zone low-contrast and even-toned — no competing textures or details | ✅ |
| **CTA button area** | Creates a small, very clean zone (typically lower third or right side) with flat color or simple background | ✅ |
| **Logo placement area** | Keeps a corner or edge zone clean and uncluttered | ✅ |
| **No overlay — standalone** | Image designed to be seen without any UI elements — full compositional complexity allowed | ✅ |

### 8.5 Conversion Tone
#### 🎯 The emotional job of the image — what it should make someone do

> **In plain English:** Every image on a website or social feed has a job beyond looking good — it needs to make the viewer feel something that leads to an action. This setting tells the AI what emotional register to aim for, which influences everything from facial expression to color temperature to subject energy.

| Tone | What it instructs the AI | Best used for | Pro |
|------|--------------------------|---------------|-----|
| **Aspirational** | Subject or scene represents an elevated, desirable version of life — slightly idealized, warm, confident | Hero sections, brand storytelling, lifestyle products | ✅ |
| **Trust / Credibility** | Clean, professional, grounded — nothing flashy. Conveys competence and reliability | B2B, professional services, healthcare, finance | ✅ |
| **Urgency** | Dynamic composition, high energy, forward motion — the image implies something is happening now | Sales, launches, limited-time offers | ✅ |
| **Calm / Frictionless** | Soft, open, inviting — low visual noise, gentle palette, relaxed subject energy | Checkout pages, onboarding, wellness, SaaS | ✅ |
| **Bold / Scroll-stopper** | High contrast, unexpected composition, strong graphic impact — designed to interrupt scroll | Ads, social campaigns, announcements | ✅ |
| **Lifestyle / Emotional** | Warm, human, relatable moment — the viewer sees themselves in the image | DTC brands, food, fashion, consumer apps | ✅ |
