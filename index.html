02 · Camera

Choose your camera

The camera defines the overall character of your image: color science, resolution, and sensor quality.

Enhance tip: In Enhance Only mode the AI preserves your photo's sensor look. You can still select a camera to request a specific color science as an enhancement direction.
No camera knowledge needed. Each camera produces a distinct look. The Kronos is warm and natural, trusted for portraits and advertising. The Fujix is medium format: larger sensor, richer tones, the "expensive" look.
03 · Lens

Choose your lens

The lens determines how much of the scene you capture and how beautifully the background blurs.

Enhance tip: Choose a lens to request a specific depth of field effect. A longer focal length (85mm f/1.2) adds background blur; a wider one keeps everything sharp.
Focal length = perspective. 24mm fits the whole environment. 50mm sees like the human eye. 85mm flatters portraits: slight compression, beautiful blur.

06 · Exposure

Control the exposure

Aperture controls depth of field. ISO controls grain and sensitivity. Motion style controls how movement appears.

Enhance tip: ISO and motion are read from your original photo. Use aperture to request a specific depth of field rendering in the enhancement.
f/1.2 = maximum blur. f/2.8 = everything in focus. ISO 100 = clean and sharp. ISO 400 = works in lower light. No shutter speed; use Motion Style instead.
